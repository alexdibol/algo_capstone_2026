# Algorithmic Trading Systems Lab 2026

## Repository Description

This repository contains the companion materials for **Algorithmic Trading Systems Lab: Five Agentic Architectures Across Equities, Oil Futures, FX Carry, Credit, and Volatility**, authored by **Alejandro Reynoso**.

The project is a pedagogical laboratory for teaching **algorithmic trading systems under governance**. It combines a handbook with five Google Colab notebooks that implement specialized agentic trading architectures across different market domains.

The central idea of the repository is that serious algorithmic trading is not merely a matter of prediction, signal generation, or backtested performance. A serious trading system must be designed as a governed architecture: its data lineage must be preserved, its assumptions must be explicit, its agents must be bounded, its gates must be inspectable, its outputs must be auditable, and its final recommendations must remain subject to human accountability.

## Contents

This repository includes:

- `HANDBOOK.pdf`  
  The full pedagogical handbook introducing the governance-first framework for agentic algorithmic trading systems.

- `notebooks/`  
  Five Google Colab notebooks providing full agentic implementations of specialized algorithmic trading systems.

The notebooks illustrate governed algorithmic system design across:

1. Equity markets  
2. Oil futures markets  
3. FX carry trading  
4. Credit markets  
5. Derivatives, volatility, and Greeks-based exposure systems  

## Pedagogical Purpose

This repository is intended as a teaching tool for courses and workshops on algorithmic trading, artificial intelligence in finance, agentic systems, and governance-first financial technology.

The materials are designed to help students move from conceptual understanding to executable experimentation. The notebooks show how specialized agents, deterministic controls, validation gates, audit artifacts, risk constraints, stress tests, and human-review procedures can be integrated into algorithmic trading workflows.

These materials are not presented as production trading systems, investment recommendations, or instructions to execute live trades. They are controlled laboratory examples for understanding how governed algorithmic systems can be designed, tested, documented, and reviewed.
